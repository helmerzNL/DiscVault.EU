Privacy Policy

DiscVault is built privacy-first. Your collection lives on your device and, if you choose, on a server you host yourself. This policy explains exactly what the DiscVault iOS app does and does not do with your data.

English Nederlands

Last updated: 5 July 2026  ·  Applies to: DiscVault for iOS and iPadOS

In short: the DiscVault app does not run any analytics, advertising, or third-party tracking. It requires no account to use. Your library is stored on your device, optionally synced to your own self-hosted DiscVault server, and film/series metadata is fetched from our MovieVault service using only a barcode or search term — never your collection or your identity.

1. Who we are

This app is published by DiscVault — VaultStack (“DiscVault”, “we”, “us”). For any privacy question or request, contact us at privacy@discvault.eu. For the purposes of the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), DiscVault — VaultStack is the data controller for the limited processing described in Section 5 (metadata lookups). For data stored on a server you host yourself, you are the controller.

2. Our privacy principles

  • Local-first. The app works fully offline, storing your library on your device.
  • No account required. You can use the app without signing up or giving us any personal details.
  • No tracking. No analytics SDKs, no advertising, no profiling, no data brokers.
  • You control your server. Any sync is to a DiscVault server that you host and administer.
  • Data minimisation. The one online service the app calls (MovieVault) receives only what it needs to answer “what disc is this?”.

3. Data stored on your device

The following is stored locally on your iPhone or iPad and is not sent to us:

  • Your library — the titles, box sets, disc formats, watchlist, and watch history you add.
  • App preferences — display and filtering settings you choose.
  • Credentials in the iOS Keychain — if you connect a server, your DiscVault server URL and personal API key; and, for metadata lookups, a device-only cryptographic key, a randomly generated device identifier, and a service token (see Section 5). These are stored securely and are only accessible to the app on this device.

Deleting the app removes this local data from your device. Local data is included in your device’s standard encrypted iCloud/iTunes device backup if you have that enabled; that backup is under your control, not ours.

4. Data you send to your own DiscVault server

DiscVault is designed to be self-hosted. If you enter your server’s address and a personal API key in Settings, the app can sync your library, watchlist, and watch history to and from your own DiscVault server. In that case:

  • The data goes directly from the app to the server you control — it does not pass through us.
  • You (or your server’s administrator) are responsible for that server, its security, and its data.
  • Connecting a server is entirely optional; the app works without it.

5. Metadata lookups (MovieVault)

To automatically fill in a title’s name, year, cover art, and format, the app contacts our MovieVault metadata service at https://movies.vaultstack.eu over HTTPS. This is the only online service the app itself relies on. It is used in two situations: when you scan a barcode, and when you search for a title by name while adding it manually.

What is sent to MovieVault:

  • The barcode number you scan, or the search text you type.
  • A randomly generated device identifier and a device public key that the app creates on first use, plus the app name, app version, and platform (“ios”). These let the service issue a token and rate-limit abuse.

What is not sent to MovieVault:

  • Your name, email address, Apple ID, or any account.
  • Your library, watchlist, or watch history.
  • Your DiscVault server address or API key.
  • Your device’s advertising identifier, location, or contacts.

The device identifier is random and not derived from you or your hardware; it is not linked to your real-world identity. MovieVault aggregates metadata from public sources server-side and returns a single result to the app. Legal basis under the GDPR: our legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)) in providing the lookup feature you requested and protecting the service from abuse. We do not use this data for advertising or profiling.

6. Camera

The app asks for camera access only so you can scan a disc’s barcode when adding a title. Scanning is performed on your device using Apple’s built-in VisionKit scanner. Camera images are processed on-device and are not stored by the app or transmitted anywhere; only the decoded barcode number is used for a lookup (see Section 5). You can decline camera access and add titles manually instead.

7. What we do not do

  • We do not collect analytics or usage statistics.
  • We do not show advertising or use advertising identifiers.
  • We do not use third-party tracking or profiling SDKs.
  • We do not sell or share your data with data brokers.
  • We do not access your location, contacts, photos, or microphone.

8. Data retention

We do not retain a copy of your library. Local data remains on your device until you delete it or uninstall the app. Data on a self-hosted server is retained according to how you configure that server. MovieVault keeps only the minimal operational records (such as the device token and short-lived request logs) needed to run and secure the lookup service, for no longer than necessary.

9. International transfers

MovieVault is hosted in the European Union. If you use the app from outside the EU, your barcode/search request is processed on EU-based infrastructure.

10. Children’s privacy

DiscVault is a general-audience collection tool and is not directed at children. It does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

11. Your rights

Because the app is local-first and we hold almost no personal data about you, most data control is directly in your hands: delete titles in the app, or uninstall to remove everything locally. Where the GDPR applies, you have rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, and portability regarding any data we do process. To exercise these rights, or to reset your MovieVault device link, contact privacy@discvault.eu. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority (in the Netherlands, the Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens).

12. Third-party metadata and artwork

Titles, descriptions, and cover art returned by MovieVault originate from public third-party sources and belong to their respective owners. They are provided to help you catalogue discs you own and are shown for identification purposes only.

13. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected on this page with a new “Last updated” date. Continued use of the app after an update means you accept the revised policy.
