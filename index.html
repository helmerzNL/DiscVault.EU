DiscVault 26 is here.
Built for the next shelf.

DiscVault 26 can be installed and used today. It brings a new desktop-first library, richer detail pages, PostgreSQL foundations, plugins, migration tools, and better admin controls for collectors who want to keep their physical media catalog self-hosted.

DiscVault 26

A new generation of DiscVault, already usable while the roadmap keeps moving.

DiscVault 26 is the next foundation for the project. It keeps the self-hosted collector focus, but modernizes the app shell, database model, admin area, plugin runtime, and migration path.

Available now

New library experience

Browse posters, switch to list or detail views, filter by format, jump into containers and box sets, and keep the interface fast on large collections.

Available now

Richer movie and container pages

Detail pages now make room for backdrops, posters, videos, metadata, collection membership, watched state, and box-set contents.

Available now

PostgreSQL and migration assistant

DiscVault 26 uses a PostgreSQL-backed model and includes a guided path to inspect and import existing DiscVault data while keeping media files in place.

Available now

Plugin-centered platform

Manage metadata sources, receivers, import sources, digital media sources, plugin packages, health checks, and background plugin jobs from the admin area.

Available now

Personal lists and watch history

Keep a personal watchlist and watched history that follows your account across devices, with poster, list, and detail layouts.

Available now

Admin, RBAC, API and MCP

DiscVault 26 adds invite-only flows, owner/admin tools, Basic and Advanced RBAC, role simulation, audit views, API access, and MCP-ready integrations.

Features

Built for people with real shelves, real stacks, and real editions.

Scan and enrich

Use your camera to scan barcodes, then automatically pull movie metadata and cover art.

Filter and search

Search by title, genre, or director and filter by format in a clean collection view.

Backup & restore

Create and download full backups of your collection, and restore them anytime with a single click.

Works offline

Add DiscVault to your homescreen and use it offline — browse and manage your collection without an internet connection.

Safe by design

Run self-hosted, control your own data path, and keep your collection data completely private.

MCP ready

Connect AI assistants to your library through DiscVault's MCP endpoint for automation and search.

v2.1

New in 2.1

User management

Add multiple users with their own accounts and permissions to share your DiscVault instance.

New in 2.1

Groups

Organize your collection into custom groups and let multiple users collaborate on shared lists.

New in 2.1

Passkey recovery

Securely recover your account using passkeys — no passwords or emails required.

v2.5

New in 2.5

MemberGroups

Any user can create their own group and invite others by username. Invited users receive a notification and can choose to join or decline. Separate from admin-managed groups — fully user-driven collaboration.

New in 2.5

Watchlist & watch history

Mark titles as watched, track when you last viewed them, and keep a personal watchlist for discs you want to get to next.

New in 2.5

Multiple languages

DiscVault is now available in French, German, and Spanish — with more languages on the way.

v2.5.1 – v2.5.3

New in 2.5.1

Invite-only registration

Disable open registration and require admin-issued invites. New users receive a one-time password to register their passkey — no sign-up form needed.

New in 2.5.2

Push notifications

Group members receive a push notification on their device whenever a new disc is added to a shared group — works on any device with DiscVault installed as a PWA.

New in 2.5.3

User-scoped MCP access

Generate a personal API key in your profile and use it to connect your AI assistant to your own collection, watchlist, and watch history — without ever exposing data from other users.

v3.0.0

New in 3.0.0

Edition & version tracking

Record which edition of a disc you own — Steelbook, Director's Cut, Limited Edition, or standard release. Essential for collectors who own multiple versions of the same title.

New in 3.0.0

Compare with Plex / Jellyfin

See which physical discs you also have digitally in Plex or Jellyfin — and which titles you only own physically but haven't ripped yet.

New in 3.0.0 beta

MovieVault metadata enrichment

Opt in to enrich releases and box sets with community-sourced metadata. Corrections you add are contributed back to MovieVault, while existing DiscVault workflows stay unchanged.

Planned for DiscVault 26

Previously announced features are still on the DiscVault 26 roadmap.

Collection & metadata

Planned for 26

Import your existing collection

Already tracking your discs in a spreadsheet or Letterboxd? Import a CSV or Letterboxd export and have your full collection in DiscVault without scanning everything from scratch.

Planned for 26

Collection statistics

Get a dashboard overview of your collection — disc count by format, genre breakdown, total runtime, and growth over time.

Planned for 26

Custom tags & lists

Add freeform tags to any title — “lent out”, “wishlist”, “gift” — and build personal lists that go beyond automatic groups.

Planned for 26

Wishlist

Keep track of discs you want to add to your collection — special editions you're hunting for, or titles you've ordered but haven't received yet. Move them to your collection with one tap once they arrive.

Planned for 26

Region indicator

Log the region of any disc — A/B/C for Blu-ray, regions 1–6 for DVD. Useful for import collectors and anyone with multiple players.

Discovery & recommendations

Planned for 26

“What to watch tonight” picker

Set a few filters — genre, runtime, mood — and DiscVault picks a random title from your own shelf. Stop scrolling streaming services when the answer is already on your wall.

Planned for 26

Personal rating

Give any title your own star rating or score, separate from the IMDb score. Filter and sort by what you think is great — not just what the crowd says.

Planned for 26

Sort by IMDb / TMDb score

The metadata is already there — use it. Sort your collection by rating to instantly surface the best titles on your shelf.

Loan & wishlist management

Planned for 26

Loan tracker

Track which discs you've lent out and to whom. See all outstanding loans at a glance and add a return reminder so nothing goes missing.

Social

Planned for 26

Public profile page

Optionally share a read-only view of your collection with anyone via a link — no account required on their end.

Planned for 26

Watchlist requests

Friends in a shared group can flag a title they want to watch. The owner gets a notification and can make the disc available.

Integrations

Planned for 26

Webhooks & notifications

Send a webhook event when a disc is added, a backup completes, or a loan is overdue — ready to connect with Home Assistant, ntfy, or any automation tool.

Planned for 26

Letterboxd sync

Automatically sync your watch history and ratings with Letterboxd, so your collection manager and film diary always stay in step.
